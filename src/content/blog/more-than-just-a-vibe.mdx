There's a moment in every project when the first design lands and the room goes
quiet for a second, then someone says "oh, that's nice." The mockup looks great.
Everyone relaxes. That feeling has a name around here: the vibe.

We love that moment too. But it's the easy part, and honestly the cheapest part
to make. "More than just a vibe" is how we remind ourselves that looking good is
where the work starts, not where it ends. Here's what's underneath.

## Decisions you can defend

Every choice — layout, copy, color, the order of the sections — should trace
back to a reason. Who is this for? What do we want them to do? When a client
points at something and asks why it's there, "it looked nice" is not an answer
we want to give. A vibe is a feeling. A good site is a series of decisions you
can explain.

That's not bureaucracy, it's how you make a second draft. When you know why a
section exists, you know how to change it without breaking the logic of the
page. When you don't, every edit is a guess.

## Speed and accessibility are features

A beautiful page that loads slowly or shuts people out isn't beautiful, it's
broken. Half your visitors are on a phone with two bars of signal. Some are
using a keyboard, or a screen reader, or a browser zoomed to 200%. If the page
only works for the person who saw the polished mockup on a 27-inch monitor, it
doesn't really work.

So performance and accessibility aren't polish we bolt on at the end. They're
constraints we design inside from the first sketch. It's a lot easier to build a
fast, open page than to rescue a slow, closed one after the fact.

## It has to hold up after launch

Launch day is the easy day. Everything is fresh, nothing has been touched, and
the site looks exactly the way we shipped it. The real test comes about six
months later.

Can someone on the team add a page without the layout falling apart? Does it
still load fast after a year of content piling up? Is it still sending people
where you wanted them to go? A site built only to look good on launch day starts
to sag the moment real life touches it.

Building for the quiet Tuesday six months out — not just the launch — is the
difference between a vibe and the real thing. The vibe gets you the first "oh,
that's nice." The work underneath is what's still standing when nobody's
looking.
